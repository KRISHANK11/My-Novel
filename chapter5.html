Chapter V

The Shadow's Reach

The apartment was quiet. Too quiet. Kairo stood near the window, watching the city lights flicker across the glass like distant stars. Behind him, Korosu sat at the table surrounded by printed pages from the broken directory—half-code, half-prophecy, all of it confusing. The silence felt wrong.

Then the shadows moved. The lights flickered once. The window exploded inward. Glass scattered across the floor as black mist surged into the room, swirling together until it formed a towering figure. Its body twisted unnaturally, limbs bending where no joints should exist. Its surface rippled like smoke forced into the shape of flesh. It had no eyes. Yet its focus locked instantly onto Kairo’s chest.

“The locket…” Korosu whispered.

The creature lunged. Kairo reacted instinctively. The Scythe of Ashen Light burst from the locket in a surge of blue fire. But the moment it formed, reality struck. This wasn’t the void. The weapon was heavy. Crushingly heavy. Kairo’s arms dropped under its weight. His muscles strained just to keep the blade upright. The heat from the scythe burned against his palms as if he were gripping a piece of living flame.

The monster struck. Kairo barely raised the weapon in time before the impact sent him crashing into the table.

“Kairo!” Korosu shouted.

Pain shot through his arms. Kairo forced himself up, gripping the scythe tighter. Blue fire erupted along the blade. Too much. The flames burst outward uncontrollably, scorching the carpet and licking up the sides of nearby furniture. Heat blasted through the room as smoke began to fill the air. Kairo staggered. The scythe trembled violently in his hands. His palms burned. His fingers shook. The weapon felt like it was draining something deeper than strength—something colder, buried inside his bones. He wasn’t fighting the monster. He was fighting the scythe itself.

Then the creature stopped. The black mist around its body shivered as it stared at the blade. Recognition. Fear.

“The Chosen One…” it hissed.

Before Kairo could strike, the creature collapsed into itself. Its body dissolved into black vapor that rushed back through the shattered window and vanished into the night. Gone.

Silence filled the apartment. The room was ruined. Burned carpet. Scorched walls. Smoke drifting toward the ceiling. The scythe flickered once and shrank back into the locket around Kairo’s neck. Kairo’s knees buckled. He caught himself on the edge of the table, breathing heavily. His hands were shaking uncontrollably, and when he looked down he saw faint burns across his palms. Even after the scythe disappeared, a cold ache lingered in his arms. He hadn’t defeated the monster. He had barely survived it.

They didn’t stay. By morning it was obvious—the apartment wasn’t safe anymore. If one shadow creature had found him, others eventually would too. They turned to the only clue they had left. The printed page pinned above the desk.

Korosu spent hours staring at the strange symbols and numbers, rubbing his tired eyes as he tried to break their meaning. Nothing worked. Until exhaustion finally broke him. Korosu flopped backward onto the bed. The impact shook the wall. The paper slipped loose and fluttered to the floor. Korosu stared down at it. Then his eyes widened.

“…Wait.” He sat upright instantly. “They’re coordinates.”

Kairo looked up.

“GPS coordinates,” Korosu said, grabbing the paper. “Latitude and longitude. The symbols were just Velran’s encryption system.”

Kairo frowned. “So it wasn’t broken code?”

Korosu shook his head. “No. Velran disguised the coordinates using mathematical notation. Anyone else would assume it’s corrupted data.” He pointed to the symbols. “See? The delta, theta, psi markers—they’re just placeholders hiding the actual numbers.”

Kairo stared at the page again. “So this leads somewhere.”

Korosu nodded. “A location.” A destination. East. Toward the rising sun. They both understood. The First Doctrine was waiting there.

Three. Three years before the destruction of Earth. Three years to find the Doctrines. Three years to stop something older than humanity. It sounded like a long time. But Kairo knew better. Three years wasn’t safety. It was preparation. A war measured in time instead of battles.

They filed a leave of absence from college that same day. When Korosu’s father called, they lied. Just a short college research trip. One week. No one else could know. By nightfall, they were already gone.

The journey took hours. City lights faded behind them, replaced by empty highways and silent hills. Eventually the roads turned rough, then nearly vanished entirely. Mountains rose around them like jagged scars in the earth. The GPS signal weakened. Then locked into place.

They stopped at a rocky hillside covered in rusted metal fragments and broken stone. At first glance, it looked like nothing. Then Kairo saw the door. A massive steel entrance embedded into the rock. Old. Hidden. Waiting.

As Kairo approached, the locket pulsed. Once. Twice. A low vibration echoed through the ground. Hidden locks disengaged deep within the mountain. With a grinding metallic sound, the door slowly slid open. It recognized him.

They stepped inside. Lights flickered to life one by one as they descended into the bunker. The underground space stretched farther than either of them expected. Servers. Consoles. Walls lined with dormant technology. Dust covered everything, but beneath it the systems still hummed faintly with power. This wasn’t just a hideout. This was a laboratory. Velran’s laboratory.

Korosu immediately moved toward the central console, plugging his laptop into the system ports. Code scrolled across the screens. He froze for a moment.

“Kairo… you need to see this.”

Kairo stepped closer. “The First Doctrine isn’t a weapon,” Korosu explained, scanning the files rapidly. “It’s a database.” He opened several folders. Ancient manuscripts appeared beside advanced simulations. Research files. Encrypted scrolls. Energy models. “This whole facility is basically Velran’s research archive,” Korosu continued. “The Doctrines aren’t objects—they’re knowledge systems. Power that has to be understood before it can be used.”

Kairo stared at the screens. “So we can’t just take it.”

“No,” Korosu said. “We have to learn it.” He leaned back in the chair. “Good news is… this place is perfect for that.”

The bunker was hidden deep underground. Shielded. Protected. If shadow creatures were hunting Kairo, this was the safest place on Earth.

They set up camp. Hours later, Korosu finally collapsed beside the console, laptop still glowing as lines of code ran across the screen. Kairo remained awake. When the internal systems registered dawn, he walked into a wide training chamber buried deep within the facility. He couldn't summon the Scythe of Ashen Light. So he took a heavy wooden spar. The weight nearly dropped him to one knee. He swung once. Slow. Unsteady. Again. And again. Each movement hurt. Each second felt heavier than the last. But Kairo kept going.

Three years. The thought settled quietly in his mind. Three years to master a weapon that nearly crushed him. Three years to understand Velran’s Doctrines. Three years before the war reached its end. This wasn’t something he would conquer overnight. It would take time. Training. Failure. But he would be ready.

Kairo stood still at last, breathing heavily as blue fire flickered along the scythe’s edge. He wasn’t the frightened orphan from before. He wasn’t the boy hiding from the world anymore. He was a warrior preparing for a three-year war.
