Chapter 1: The Day the Flame Stirred

 kairo’s mornings were always quiet.
The house was cold, the air still. His parents left early for work, and he was used to the silence. Alone at the breakfast table, he stared blankly at his cereal as the clock ticked on. Around his neck, the black locket — shaped like a curved scythe — rested heavy against his chest.

His father gave it to him on his seventh birthday.
"Never take it off, no matter what," he had said.
Back then, kairo didn’t ask questions. Now, he wished he had.

That Afternoon

The classroom door opened slowly. A teacher entered, eyes filled with hesitation. She called kairo out into the hallway. He followed, confused. Waiting.

Then the words hit him.

“There was an accident… your parents didn’t survive.”

The world stopped.

No screams. No reaction. Just numbness.
As if everything inside him shut down.
The locket against his chest pulsed faintly — once, like a heartbeat not his own.

Days Later

The funeral was silent. No relatives came.
Within the week, kairo was moved to a government-funded orphanage on the city outskirts.

His old neighborhood friend, Rivan, arrived a few days later — his parents had moved overseas and left him behind. It was the only familiar face kairo recognized, but even that didn’t matter much anymore.

He didn’t speak unless necessary.
Didn’t eat unless forced.
All he did was sit in the corner, clutching the locket tighter each day.

The Incident

The orphanage wasn’t kind.
Boys twice his size treated him like a target. One afternoon, a larger kid approached while the caretakers weren’t watching.

“What’s with the creepy necklace, huh?”

kairo didn’t answer.

“Fine. I’ll take it myself.”

The boy grabbed the locket.

He didn’t even have time to blink before his palm ignited in searing blue fire. He screamed and collapsed to the floor, crying as the skin sizzled.

Panic erupted. Children ran. Staff rushed in. No one understood what happened.
Except kario.

Because the moment the bully’s hand touched it — the locket had awakened.

The Choice

Later that night, alone in his room, Kairo sat on the floor, trembling.

The locket was glowing faintly — soft pulses of light, like it was alive. Something inside it was trying to speak. Trying to move.

“What are you?” he whispered.
“Why did you burn him?”

He clutched it with both hands. The air around him grew warmer. The room darkened at the edges. His breathing slowed. Everything told him to drop it. But he didn’t.

Instead, slowly, he pulled the chain from around his neck.

The moment the locket left his skin, a shockwave burst from it, blinding him.

The Gate Opens

He fell backward — but not onto the floor.
He was floating.

Around him, endless void. Dark space. Burning stars scattered across a dead sky. The locket hovered in front of him, glowing violently.

A voice echoed through the dark.

“So... you finally let go.”

“You, who carries the mark of the Scythe…”

“You are the Chosen One.”

kairo's breath caught in his throat. He reached toward the voice. But there was no one there.

Only the light.
And the fire inside it.
