Chapter XI

The Dead World

The portal didn’t open .

It broke .

Space twisted—and threw them through .

Kairo hit the ground hard . But the impact felt wrong . Too light . His body bounced slightly before settling . Korosu crashed beside him, coughing .

“What—what is this—”

The air burned going in . Dry . Thin . Like breathing dust .

Kairo pushed himself up fast . Every movement felt delayed . His body moved—but the ground didn’t fully respond . Low gravity . Unstable . Dangerous .

He looked up . Open land . No cover .

“Move.”

He grabbed Korosu and pulled him low . They ran . Or tried to . Each step carried them too far, their balance constantly shifting . Half-running, half-floating .

Solen moved ahead of them . Perfect . Silent . Untouched by the world around him .

They reached a cluster of massive rocks and pressed against them . Breathing uneven . Waiting . Seconds passed . Nothing came . No sound . No movement .

Kairo exhaled slowly . “…We’re not seen.” Barely .

He looked out . And went still .

The world was dead . Not destroyed . Not ruined . Just… empty . Endless brown terrain stretched in every direction . Dust storms moved sideways across the land, cutting through the air like curtains . Black rock formations rose from the ground—sharp, uneven, unnatural .

Then—the castle .

Kairo’s focus locked . It wasn’t just massive . It was overwhelming . Dark stone rising into the sky until it disappeared into the storm above . No windows . No light . Just presence . Like it existed to crush everything beneath it .

Beside it—a settlement . Smaller, but still large . Clusters of structures carved from the same dark rock . Cold . Silent .

And between them—movement . Dozens of shadow figures . No chaos . No randomness . They were working .

Korosu carefully opened his laptop . “Please work…” The screen flickered . Lagged . Then stabilized . He exhaled . “Okay… got it.”

He started scanning . Tracking . Zooming in . “They’re mining,” he said .

Kairo narrowed his eyes . The shadows dug deep into the ground, pulling something out . A substance . Dark . Faintly glowing . It pulsed softly . Like it was alive .

Solen spoke . “That is what keeps him breathing.”

Kairo didn’t look away . “The Destroyer?”

“Yes.” Solen’s voice remained steady . “He is not truly immortal . Not without the Origin Crystal .”

Korosu glanced up . “So this thing—”

“—is sustaining him,” Solen finished . “Without it, he decays .”

“How fast?”

“Days . Perhaps hours .”

Silence fell . Kairo stared at the mining operation again . The endless digging . The constant movement . This wasn’t dominance . It was survival .

“…He’s dependent,” Kairo said quietly .

No one disagreed .

“We move closer,” Kairo said .

Korosu turned immediately . “That’s not a plan—that’s suicide .”

Kairo had already started moving . Solen followed without a word . Korosu stared at them, then grabbed his laptop . “…I hate both of you .”

They moved low . Careful . Rock to rock . Using dust storms as cover . Every step had to be controlled . The gravity kept pulling them off rhythm .

The castle grew larger . Heavier . More suffocating . They could hear it now . The settlement . Voices . Not human . But structured . Alive .

Then—a figure stepped out . Directly in front of them .

Kairo stopped instantly . His hand moved toward the locket . The figure froze too . Not a shadow . Something else . Smaller . Wrapped in worn layers of cloth . Its face partially hidden .

But its eyes—wide . Alert . It saw them . All of them .

Time stretched . One second too long .

Then—it moved . Fast . It grabbed Kairo’s arm . Grip tight . Urgent .

“Wait—!”

Korosu stepped forward—too late . The figure pulled them behind the rocks—then down .

They dropped into darkness . Sliding through a narrow passage . Until—they landed .

A small underground chamber . Dim light filled the space—glowing stones embedded into the walls . The air felt thicker here . Easier . Safer .

The figure stood in front of them . Breathing fast . Watching them closely . Fear in its eyes . But something else too . Recognition .

Its gaze locked onto Kairo’s chest . The locket . It stepped closer . Slowly . Then spoke .

The language was wrong . Unfamiliar .

Korosu frowned . “I don’t understand that .”

Solen didn’t move . Didn’t react . Then slowly—he turned to Kairo .

“It says…” A pause . “…it has been waiting .”

Kairo’s eyes narrowed . “For us? ”

Solen shook his head . “No .” His voice dropped . “For the scythe .”
