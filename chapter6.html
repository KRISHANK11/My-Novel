Chapter VI

The Weight of Calm

The training hall was silent . The underground chamber stretched wide beneath the bunker, its ceiling disappearing into shadows far above . Dust drifted slowly through thin beams of artificial light .

Kairo stood alone in the center of the room . In his hands was a long wooden spar . It was thick, worn, and heavier than a normal training staff—Korosu had specifically chosen one that forced Kairo to strain his arms .

Kairo swung it again . The wood sliced through the air with a dull whump . Again . His shoulders burned . Again . His hands trembled . Sweat soaked through his shirt as the staff swung in wide arcs across the empty hall .

He imagined the scythe . The long blade . The curved reach . The deadly momentum . But imagination didn’t make him stronger . The staff slipped slightly in his grip . He corrected it and swung again .

Minutes passed . Then an hour . Finally his arms gave out . The staff dropped against the floor with a heavy thud . Kairo bent forward, breathing hard, sweat gripping onto the cold concrete beneath his feet .

He touched the locket around his neck . Cold . Silent . Nothing . Kairo clenched his jaw . He had fought a monster . Held the Scythe of Ashen Light . But standing here now, he felt like a pretender . Like someone playing the role of a warrior instead of actually being one .

From across the hall, a voice echoed . “Kairo .”

Korosu stood near one of the lab’s medical terminals, waving him over . The glowing screens behind him hummed quietly with activity . Kairo grabbed a towel from the floor and walked toward him . Korosu turned toward Kairo . “Sit .” Kairo raised an eyebrow but obeyed . Korosu began attaching small sensors to his chest and temples .

“What are you doing?” Kairo asked .

“Bio-feedback,” Korosu replied . “Heart rate . Brain activity . Stress response .”

Kairo frowned . “Why ?”

Korosu tapped a few keys . Numbers instantly appeared across the screen . “Because I think I figured out what happened during the fight in your apartment .” He turned the monitor so Kairo could see it . “Your heart rate during the attack peaked at one hundred and eighty beats per minute .”

Kairo blinked . “That’s… bad ?”

“That’s panic mode,” Korosu said . “Your body thought it was about to die .”

Kairo leaned back in the chair . “So ?”

Korosu pointed at the locket . “You didn’t summon the scythe .”

Kairo frowned . “What ?”

“Your terror did .”

The words hung in the air . Korosu continued . “When the shadow creature attacked, your fear triggered the weapon . But because you were terrified, the scythe became unstable .”

He typed something else into the console . “Think of it like trying to lift a weight while drowning . Your mind was fighting itself .”

Kairo remembered the fight . The crushing weight . The uncontrollable flames . “So fear made it heavier?” he asked .

Korosu nodded . “Exactly .” He folded his arms . “Fear is dead weight . It’s about control .” Korosu nodded . “You and the weapon need to move as one system,” Korosu explained . “Minimal fear . Maximum patience .”

He tapped the screen again . “You don’t force the scythe to appear . You have to mentally be stable, so you need meditate and do some exercise .” Kairo slowly sat down .

Kairo trained his mind . Morning: wooden spar practice . Night: meditation . Slowly, his breathing improved . His thoughts became quieter . Not empty . Just calmer . Every day Kairo trained his body while Korosu researched the First Doctrine .

On the sixth night, the bunker was completely silent . Korosu had fallen asleep at his desk, surrounded by glowing monitors . In the training hall, Kairo sat motionless . Breathing slowly . In . Out .

The locket around his neck began to glow faintly . Blue . Kairo opened his eyes . He raised his hand . Not out of fear . Not out of desperation . Just intention .

The air shifted . A quiet sapphire light formed in front of him . The Scythe of Ashen Light appeared . This time there was no violent explosion . No uncontrolled flames . Only a steady hum .

Kairo wrapped his hand around the handle . The weapon was still heavy . But it didn’t crush him . His arms trembled slightly, but he stayed standing . The blue glow filled the chamber . Korosu woke up .

Korosu stepped slowly into the training hall . The blue glow from the scythe illuminated the room . For a moment he simply stared . “…You summoned it,” he said quietly .

Kairo nodded . The weapon was still heavy in his grip, but this time it didn’t crush him . Korosu turned toward the research monitors still glowing behind him . “I’m still decoding Velran’s archives,” he said . “But I think I understand the beginning of it .”

Kairo looked at him . Korosu crossed his arms . “The First Doctrine isn’t a power .” He pointed at the scythe . “It’s the foundation . The ability to exist with the weapon… without letting it destroy you .”

Kairo looked down at the blade . The blue light reflected in his eyes . Korosu continued . “Velran didn’t design the Doctrines to be unlocked quickly .” He gestured toward the bunker . “They’re meant to shape a warrior over years .”

Kairo tightened his grip on the scythe . Three years . Three years to grow strong enough to face what was coming . The scythe hummed softly in his hand . For the first time—it no longer felt like an enemy . It felt like the beginning .

And the training had only just begun .
