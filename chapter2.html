Chapter II

The Awakening Flame

Kairo’s body stirred. It wasn’t a regular awakening . His senses felt like they were stretched thin, tightened, sharpened beyond human limits . The air smelled… ancient . The stone floor beneath him pulsed faintly . He sat up, clutching his chest—his locket, still hanging from his neck, was glowing a pale, icy blue . He looked around . This wasn’t his room . Not even Earth . He stood inside a colossal hallway, its walls alive and shifting—like memories trying to remember themselves .

He walked forward, instinctively . The hallway bent and warped like a living creature, reshaping its insides until it opened into a grand, circular chamber . Dusty bookshelves formed a spiral, a dark library filled with knowledge never meant to be read . In the center stood a lone figure . Cloaked in black, its face hidden under a hood, it held two jet-black rods in either hand .

“You want to use that scythe?” the figure’s voice rumbled, deep and hollow .

Kairo froze . “Scythe? What scythe?” he looked at his glowing locket in confusion . “I—I don’t even know how to fight!”

The figure didn’t wait . It dashed forward with inhuman speed . Kairo barely dodged the first strike, but the second slammed into his ribs . Pain exploded in his side . He gasped, fell to the floor .

“You are too weak,” the figure whispered, stepping back . Kairo coughed, wheezing . Blood dripped from his lip . The locket on his chest pulsed wildly, the glow intensifying .

Then—

FLASH.

A surge of heat . A roar inside his ears . The locket ripped itself off his chest, hovered, spun, and with a blaze of blue flame—transformed into a full scythe.

His eyes flared with an eerie blue glow . His body lifted slightly, surrounded by the aura of azure fire . He grabbed the scythe . Now he could fight .

The figure launched forward again . Kairo met the charge with a block, their weapons clashing . Sparks burst from every strike . Kairo ducked a rod swipe, spun, and landed a glancing cut on the figure’s side . The figure twisted, vanished into smoke, reappeared behind—but Kairo had already turned, slamming the back of the scythe’s blade into its stomach . Strike after strike, Kairo danced like instinct took over . He wasn’t just fighting . He was surviving . Dominating .

With a final burst of speed, Kairo slashed downward . The scythe met resistance—then broke through .

CLANG.

The figure stumbled back, kneeling . “So… you were chosen,” it muttered, dissolving into black mist .

Kairo dropped to his knees, panting . The blue flames around him vanished . His scythe shrunk, twisted, and locked itself back around his neck in its locket form . A scroll fluttered down from nowhere, gently hitting his forehead .

Kairo blinked, grabbed it . Unrolling it slowly, he read the strange runes that somehow made sense in his mind :

“To command the Scythe of Ashen Light, you must master the Five Doctrines.”

He blinked . “What the hell just happened…?”
